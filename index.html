A.P.J Abdul Kalam

(1931-2015)

Missile Man Of India

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam: The Missile Man of India Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was one of India's most illustrious scientists and a revered leader who played a crucial role in shaping India's space and missile programs. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam's journey from a humble background to becoming the 11th President of India is nothing short of inspirational. His remarkable contribution to the defense sector, particularly in missile technology, earned him the title "Missile Man of India." ### Early Life and Education Kalam hailed from a modest family, but his parents instilled in him the values of hard work and perseverance. His curiosity and thirst for knowledge were evident from an early age. After completing his schooling in Rameswaram, he pursued a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT). This education laid the foundation for his future contributions to India's aerospace advancements. ### Role in India's Missile Development Kalam’s most significant contributions to India were in the field of missile technology. He joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) during the early stages of their development. He was a key player in the development of the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980, making India one of the few nations with indigenous satellite launch capability. However, it was his leadership in the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP) that earned him the title "Missile Man." Under this program, India developed a series of successful missiles, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles, which boosted India’s defense capabilities. These missiles not only strengthened India’s military position but also established the country as a force to reckon with in the global defense arena. ### Visionary Leadership and Presidency Beyond his scientific contributions, A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was known for his visionary leadership. In 2002, he was elected as the 11th President of India, serving until 2007. Often referred to as the "People's President," Kalam inspired millions of Indians with his humility, accessibility, and deep connection with the youth. His vision for India as a developed nation by 2020 was encapsulated in his book *India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium*. He tirelessly advocated for education, particularly in science and technology, and believed that empowering the youth was the key to India's progress. ### Legacy and Inspiration

Even after his presidency, Dr. Kalam continued to work closely with students and educational institutions, delivering lectures and inspiring young minds to dream big and contribute to nation-building. His life serves as a beacon of hope and resilience, especially for those from underprivileged backgrounds, proving that dedication and hard work can overcome any challenge. Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong. His death was a great loss to the nation, but his legacy as a scientist, leader, and teacher lives on. ### Conclusion A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's contributions to India’s space and defense technology, coupled with his leadership as the President, have left an indelible mark on the nation. His journey from a small town to becoming the "Missile Man" and the "People’s President" is a testament to the power of dreams, determination, and the pursuit of excellence. Kalam's life continues to inspire millions, making him not just a hero of India, but a global icon of peace, progress, and possibility.
